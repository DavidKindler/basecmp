Building signage

A good signage system is visible, readable, appropriate and presents us with a good opportunity to communicate a clear brand identity. Ensure the quality of the signage we use around buildings is appropriate for a world-class company.

Signage types

Use either high level signs for advertising or routing signs for buildings and sites.

Sites have approved processes for designing, contracting, placing, maintaining (and removing) signs. Approach one of our preferred suppliers with a clear brief based on your requirements and the signage guidelines. Once the signs have been installed, agree upon maintenance contracts for assigning responsibility for signage maintenance and a procedure for signage removal and disposal.

High level signs

The main purpose of high level signs is to advertise the brand. The logo is the only element that can be used on a high level sign. They are placed on buildings or advertising masts. Mounted on a roof or on the wall of a building, they should stand out and be clearly visible from a distance.

High level signs should ideally only be placed at so-called ‘A locations,’ such as next to central highways, city centers, main ports (airports, train stations), etc. The definition of an ‘A location’ differs from country to country.

For more details on this subject please refer to outdoor media companies and our preferred suppliers.

A high level sign can also be placed to emphasize the importance of the building but preferably only for company, divisional or country headquarters. The advertising objective remains valid and should be taken into account to position the sign in the most effective place.

Signs should be as large as the space allows, without being over-bearing. Branding should continue throughout the site with all other signs bearing the NXP logo. For more details on this subject please refer to outdoor media companies and our preferred suppliers.

Routing signs

Routing signs are placed on or near sites and buildings for navigation to the appropriate building, department or room. They should be clear to all visitors and employees by providing confirmation of arrival and guidance to the correct destination. Routing signs can also be used to inform the visitor about site rules or special situations. However, they should never be used to communicate information that could also be addressed in another form; the less signs, the better.

Locations

The following four situations are relevant when specifying a signage system:

  • An NXP site: More than one building on an NXP owned site; only NXP tenants; at least one gate to enter the site
  • A multi-tenant site: More than one building; not all owned or occupied by NXP; at least one gate to enter the site. (Note: the site/building may well have its own overall signage system)
  • An NXP building: An NXP owned/rented building; only NXP tenant(s); the building can be standalone or located on an NXP or multi-tenant site
  • A multi-tenant building: A building in which NXP is one of the tenants but occupies one or more floors. (Note: the site/building may well have its own overall signage system)

Responsibility

Issues of responsibility are locally controlled. Generally, facility management, site management and/or country/local brand management will commission the acquisition of signage. Responsibilities should be defined clearly before a supplier is contacted.

Methodology

Routing signs should only be placed at decision spots. A letter or number code for buildings is preferred to departmental/ organizational names to avoid the cost of adapting to organizational changes.

The following directional structure for outdoor routing should be followed:
  Arrow indication: straight on
  Arrow indication: to the right
  Arrow indication: to the left

Within each directional indication the destinations should be shown in alphabetical order rather than the order of approach. The standard size of letters for outdoor signs is 60mm, which is easy to read on sites with a speed limitation of 30km/h. In exceptional cases, the preferred supplier is able to recommend deviations from the standard letter height.

Flags

We use company flags in standard sizes: 100 x 150cm, 150 x 225cm, 200 x 300cm and 300 x 450cm.

Logo

The flag must be white and display only the full-color NXP logo. Do not add any other graphic elements.

Badges

Use of the Arial font is allowed for badges. Full color is allowed on both sides or full color for the front and black & white for the back.

Vehicle livery

Company vehicles are highly visible and can help increase positive awareness of our brand. Apply the identity elements clearly and consistently to give the right impression of professionalism.

Logo

Place the NXP on the left and right sides of the vehicle, and on the front. Make sure the logo is as large as possible while respecting the clear space rules. Whenever possible, make sure the logo is not divided by door handles, sliding rails or door breaks.

Multi-colored line

The multi-colored line should run around the middle of the whole vehicle (always below the NXP logo).

Other elements

The website address should appear on the back and sides of the vehicle as indicated (to match pantone cool gray 9 or C0-M0-Y0-K70). Do not use the brand promise or another tagline on vehicles.

Colors

The base background color for all vehicles is white.
