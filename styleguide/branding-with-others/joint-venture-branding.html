Joint venture branding

Joint venture

In a joint venture branding, two companies of comparable partnership combine their efforts in a new business entity. This new venture requires a dedicated identity that respects the communication principles and brands of both parties and takes into consideration the goals and objectives of the joint venture.

Decision tree

This decision tree helps to decide which joint branding type is best suited for the joint venture. An assessment of the business risk should also be taken into account in determining the branding strategy. Ask the question, what effect would the success or failure of this joint venture have on the NXP brand and the chosen JV brand name?

Do’s and don’ts


General

A NXP Joint Venture always requires a name, a logo and an identity. These have to be described in a corporate identity document. The depth of this document can vary.

Alignment with the Corporate Communications and Branding department of NXP is required. Contact them at brand. support@nxp.com to get advice and discuss exceptions etc. Always refer to the legal department for advice regarding trademarks, trade names and domain names.

The NXP and the partner brand identity should be respected in the creation of the joint venture brand. See http://www.nxp.com/smo/branding/ for more information on the NXP brand.

Process

The process to think about the joint branding issues starts during the joint ventures negotiations and should be clear before reaching a joint venture agreement. NXP Corporate Communications & Branding can assist in this process.

It is advised to dedicate a chapter in the joint venture agreement to branding and naming.

NXP Corporate Communications & Branding should be contacted before the start of the design process for guidance on the name, logo and identity of the joint venture brand.


Be aware that creating a new brand takes time, funding and dedication.

Branding & logos

When more than two parent brands are creating the joint venture then a new brand name is clearly preferred. Only seldom is it possible to combine three or more parent names in one new joint venture name.

When more brands are stakeholders in a joint venture then their respective parent brand logos can be used in the communication to support the joint venture, see the next page for recommendations.

During a certain period it is advised to use the parent brands in the communication to support the positioning of the joint venture.

Identity

The creation of the joint venture identity has to be done with respect for the basic principles behind the parent brand identity strategies.

To avoid any confusion or dilution of the parent brands it is therefor NOT allowed to base the joint venture identity on identity elements (colors, fonts, logo, etc..) of the parent companies.

Do’s and don’ts


New name solution

There are several business arguments which can support the choice for a ‘new name.’ For instance the need for the joint venture to enter new markets or the need to develop and position a new product or service. The ‘new name’ solution always requires a new identity and logo. Developing a new brand can be done with various levels of sophistication. NXP requires its joint ventures to adhere to certain quality levels of branding and brand documentation balanced with the joint venture strategy and size. To avoid creating unclear branding (for all the parties involved), incorporating elements from the parent companies’ identities into the new joint venture corporate identity is not allowed.


NXP/Partner solution

There are several business arguments which can support the choice for keeping the parent company names. For instance the need for the joint venture to grow in existing markets or the need to strengthen existing product or service ranges.

The NXP/partner name solution always requires a new identity and logo. When combining NXP with another company name beware of over-complicating the name with a long list of acronyms. Developing a new brand can be done with various levels of sophistication. NXP requires its joint ventures to adhere to certain quality levels of branding and brand documentation balanced with the joint venture strategy and size. To avoid creating unclear branding (for all the parties involved), incorporating elements from the parent companies’ identities into the new joint venture corporate identity is not allowed.
