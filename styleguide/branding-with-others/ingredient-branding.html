Ingredient branding

The NXP ingredient logos are special use versions of our standard NXP logo and as such, many of the same guidelines apply for their use. You must never change it in any way, under any circumstances. This ensures legal protection and helps recognition and awareness worldwide.

Why do we use the ingredient logos?

Using the ingredient logos provides benefit for both NXP and our end product manufacturers. For NXP, using the ingredient logo provides exposure and helps to support and add value to our brand. For end-product manufacturers, use of the ingredient logo adds credibility and enhances their brand through association.

Ingredient logo variations

There is one primary ingredient logo, ‘Enhanced by NXP.’ It is the general version that conveys a feeling of improving the overall experience of the end product. Variations of the ingredient logo allow for additional designators to build a context for High Performance Mixed Signal technologies. Examples:

  • Technology enhanced by NXP
  • Energy efficiency enhanced by NXP
  • Safety enhanced by NXP
  • Security enhanced by NXP
  • Performance enhanced by NXP

Ingredient logo versions

As with the standard NXP logo, we have created the NXP ingredient logos in different versions:

1. FULL-COLOR LOGO: This default logo should be used on virtually all materials
2. BLACK-AND-WHITE LOGO: For use in newsprint or on a fax sheet

Minimum clear space

Our logo must always be shown in a clear and legible form. To help ensure this, please always maintain a ‘clear space’ around the logo – where no text, images or symbols may appear. The easy-to-follow rule to achieve the correct clear space is to ensure a minimum space of 50% of the height of the logo is always left clear around the NXP logo (see example).

Applications

Ingredient logos are used to enhance our brand and that of our end-product manufacturers. Some general rules on where and how to use them:

  • On the front or back of end-products
  • In end-product user interfaces
  • On the end-product packaging and user manuals
  • On print promotions and direct marketing
  • In conjunction with product highlights and product details
  • Broadcast on television, radio and web
  • Within text explaining the featured ingredient
